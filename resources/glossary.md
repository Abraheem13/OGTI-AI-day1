# 📖 Glossary — AI Terms for Oil & Gas Professionals

A no-jargon reference of the key terms used in this training.

---

## A

**Agentic AI** — AI that can plan, take actions, observe results, and self-correct across multiple steps. Unlike a chat assistant (one prompt = one answer), an agent might pursue a multi-step goal like "ensure every rig crew has up-to-date HSE certification" by querying HR systems, sending emails, and escalating exceptions.

**Anonymisation** — Removing identifying details from data before sharing it. Standard placeholders: `[NAME]`, `[FIELD]`, `[VENDOR]`, `[DATE]`, `[QUANTITY]`, `[WELL-X]`.

**API** — Application Programming Interface. A way for software to talk to other software. Most AI products offer an API for developers; you usually interact via the chat interface, not the API directly.

**Automation (rules-based)** — Software that follows fixed rules. Deterministic. "If X, then Y." Used for billing, accounting, compliance triggers. Not the same as AI.

---

## B

**BAFO** — Best And Final Offer. The last round of price negotiation in a procurement process. Never paste BAFO data into a public AI.

---

## C

**ChatGPT** — Conversational AI built by OpenAI. Fast, fluent, broad. Free tier has message limits per 3 hours. Sign-up at chat.openai.com.

**Claude** — Conversational AI built by Anthropic. Strong on long-document reading and nuanced writing. Generous free tier for PDF uploads. Sign-up at claude.ai.

**Confined Space** — In O&G, a vessel or tank with limited entry/egress requiring specific entry permits and gas testing. Used in our SOP exercises.

**Context Window** — How much text an AI can read in one go. Claude's free tier has a larger context window than ChatGPT's free tier, making it better for long documents.

**Copilot (Microsoft 365)** — AI built INTO Office apps (Outlook, Word, Excel, Teams). Different from public AI because it works with YOUR own files and emails. Requires a paid licence (~$30/user/month). Data residency depends on your tenant.

**Corrective Action** — Fix a problem that has already occurred. Pair with Preventive Action.

---

## D

**Digital Twin** — A virtual model of a physical asset (a well, a refinery unit, a pipeline) that mirrors its real-time state. Increasingly common in O&G for what-if simulation.

---

## E

**Engineering with AI** — The mindset shift from "using" AI as a tool to embedding it in how teams plan, execute, and report. The phrase used throughout this training.

---

## F

**Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)** — A structured visual for brainstorming causes of a problem, organised by category (the 6M's: Manpower, Method, Machine, Material, Measurement, Mother Nature).

**5-Part Anatomy** — Our universal prompt framework: Role · Context · Task · Format · Limits.

**5-Whys** — A root-cause analysis technique. Ask "why?" five times in succession to drill from symptom to root cause.

---

## G

**Generative AI** — AI that produces new content (text, images, code) rather than just classifying or predicting. ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot are all generative AI.

**Green List** — Content that is safe to paste into a public AI (public regulations, technical concepts, anonymised data). Counterpart to the Red List.

---

## H

**Hallucination** — When an AI produces a confident, well-formed answer that is factually wrong — including invented dates, section numbers, citations, or quotes. The single biggest risk when using AI for regulatory or technical work.

**HSE** — Health, Safety, and Environment. The professional domain responsible for protecting people and the environment in industrial operations.

---

## I

**Incident** — Any unplanned event that resulted (or could have resulted) in injury, environmental release, equipment damage, or production loss.

---

## J

**Joint Venture (JV)** — Common in upstream O&G. Two or more parties combine capital and expertise to develop a block. JV agreements are commercially sensitive — firmly Red List.

---

## K

**KPI** — Key Performance Indicator. A measurable goal (e.g. "reduce non-productive time by 25%"). AI is increasingly used to monitor and report on KPIs.

---

## L

**Large Language Model (LLM)** — The underlying technology behind ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot. Models trained on enormous amounts of text that learn to predict and generate language.

**LEL** — Lower Explosive Limit. The minimum concentration of a flammable gas in air that will burn. Critical for confined space entry.

---

## M

**Master Prompt** — A reusable, refined prompt that you save and use repeatedly. The deliverable of the Day 1 capstone activity.

---

## N

**NotebookLM** — Google's AI tool that reads ONLY the documents you upload. Every answer cites the exact page. Cannot hallucinate beyond your sources. The right tool for JV contracts, OGRA rulings, tender packs. notebooklm.google.com

---

## O

**OGRA** — Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority of Pakistan. Regulates the midstream and downstream petroleum sector. Sets pricing, licensing, and reporting requirements.

**OGTI** — Oil and Gas Training Institute, Islamabad. The training venue for this programme.

---

## P

**PECA 2016** — Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016. Pakistani legislation that, among other things, criminalises unauthorised disclosure of state-owned strategic data — including via public AI tools whose servers sit abroad.

**Perplexity** — AI-powered search engine that searches the LIVE internet and cites every source. Use it for "what's the latest…" questions. perplexity.ai

**PPRA** — Public Procurement Regulatory Authority of Pakistan. Sets the rules for public procurement, including PPRA Rule 36 (justification for single-source / urgency procurement).

**Preventive Action** — A change that stops a problem recurring. Paired with Corrective Action.

**Prompt** — The instruction or question you give an AI. Quality of prompt = quality of output.

**Prompt Engineering** — The discipline of writing effective prompts. Our 5-Part Anatomy is one framework among many.

---

## Q

**Quiz** — A short test to check understanding. Our three quizzes are in `quizzes/` as HTML files you open in any browser.

---

## R

**Red List** — Content that must NEVER be pasted into a public AI: production data, JV contracts, employee data, bid pricing, internal incidents before disclosure, etc. See `resources/red_list_green_list.md`.

**RPA (Robotic Process Automation)** — Rules-based automation of repetitive tasks like data entry, report generation, file moves. Foundational layer below AI assistants. Day 2 of this programme covers RPA.

---

## S

**SOP** — Standard Operating Procedure. A documented, step-by-step instruction for performing a task safely and consistently.

---

## T

**Tool Duel** — Our exercise of running the SAME prompt in ChatGPT AND Claude and comparing the outputs.

---

## V

**Verification** — The discipline of checking AI output against original sources before using it. Mandatory for any regulatory citation, named person, date, quantity, or quoted text.

**Vision 2030** — A common umbrella term for national digitalisation roadmaps. Used by Saudi Arabia (the original), UAE, Pakistan and others. Most include explicit AI workforce and infrastructure targets.

---

## W

**Workflow** — A sequence of related tasks that achieves an outcome (e.g. "process a vendor invoice from receipt to payment"). The right unit of analysis when deciding whether to apply Automation, AI Assistant, or Agentic AI.

---

## Z

**Zero-Day Hallucination** — When a brand-new factual error first appears in an AI output. There is no way to predict these in advance — which is why **verification** is non-negotiable for any externally-shared document.
