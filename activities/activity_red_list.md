# 🚦 Activity — The Red List Sorter

**Time required:** 15 minutes
**Format:** Groups of 3-4 at your departmental table
**Purpose:** Build muscle memory for what you can and cannot paste into public AI

---

## 🎯 The challenge

You will be shown 20 examples of content. For each one, decide **as a group**:

- 🛑 **RED** — never paste this into a public AI
- ⚠️ **AMBER** — paste only after anonymising
- ✅ **GREEN** — safe as-is

Sort them onto your table mat into three columns.

## 📊 Discussion after sorting

For each row, the facilitator will reveal the recommended answer. **More important than getting the answer right** is whether your group:

1. Caught the **identity** issue (real names, real fields, real prices)
2. Caught the **timing** issue (internal data before disclosure)
3. Caught the **classification** issue ("Partner Only", "Need-to-Know")
4. Spotted that anonymisation often solves the problem

## 💡 Three-pattern takeaway

By the end of this activity you should have internalised three patterns:

1. **Identity = Red.** Real names of people, fields, wells, vendors, contracts, prices → always anonymise first
2. **Time-sensitive = Red.** Internal data that hasn't been disclosed yet → never share with public AI
3. **Generic = Green.** Concepts, public regulations, published literature → safe

When in doubt, ask: *"if a competitor's PR team got hold of this prompt log tomorrow, would I be embarrassed?"*

If yes → don't paste it.
