# 🏆 Capstone Activity — Build Your Master Prompt

**Time required:** 30 minutes
**Format:** Small groups (3-4 people) or solo
**Deliverable:** One reusable prompt + one input + one output sample, presented to the room

---

## 🎯 The mission

By the end of this activity, every participant walks away with **ONE master prompt** they can paste at their desk tomorrow morning and save **2+ hours per week**.

This is not theory. This is the prompt that proves the day was worth your time.

---

## 📋 Step 1 — Pick a real task (5 minutes)

Choose a task that meets ALL these criteria:
- [ ] You do it at least weekly
- [ ] It currently takes you 30+ minutes
- [ ] The output is text-based (a document, summary, email, report)
- [ ] You can describe what "good" looks like
- [ ] The input data can be anonymised without losing the structure

**Examples of good capstone tasks:**

| Function | Recurring task |
|----------|----------------|
| Operations | Summarise 5 rig-daily reports into one weekly brief |
| HSE | Draft toolbox-talk topic from the past week's near-misses |
| Procurement | Draft PPRA Rule 36 justification for routine sole-source items |
| Engineering | Convert a well-test data table into an interpretation memo |
| Management | Convert raw monthly KPIs into a board summary |
| Compliance | Compare a vendor's contract clauses against our standard terms |

**Bad capstone tasks** (avoid these): tasks involving raw production numbers, named people, specific bid prices, or anything on the Red List that can't be cleanly anonymised.

---

## 📋 Step 2 — Build the 5-Part Anatomy (10 minutes)

Use a flipchart or this template. Do this **on paper first**, then transfer to the AI tool.

```
ROLE:    You are a ____________________________________
         at a ________________________________________ .

CONTEXT: ___________________________________________
         ___________________________________________
         ___________________________________________ .

TASK:    Produce a __________________________________
         covering:
         1. ________________________________
         2. ________________________________
         3. ________________________________
         4. ________________________________
         5. ________________________________

FORMAT:  ___________________________________________
         (e.g. markdown with tables / formal memo / 
         WhatsApp-ready bullets / one-page summary)

LIMITS:  - Length: ________________________________
         - Tone: ___________________________________
         - Avoid: __________________________________
         - Verification: ___________________________
         - Anti-hallucination: ____________________
```

---

## 📋 Step 3 — Test and refine (10 minutes)

1. Paste your prompt into **ChatGPT or Claude** (or both — compare)
2. Use **fictional data** from a Potwar / Kirthar / Indus Basin block — never real data
3. Read the output critically:
   - What did it get right?
   - What did it get wrong?
   - Where did it hallucinate?
4. **Refine the prompt** to fix the most painful issue
5. Run again. Repeat until 80% of the output is usable without editing.

---

## 📋 Step 4 — Present (5 minutes)

Each team or individual presents to the room:

1. **The prompt** — read it aloud, all five parts
2. **One sample input** — show the (anonymised) data you fed in
3. **One sample output** — show what the AI produced
4. **Your scorecard** — see scoring criteria below

---

## 📊 Scoring criteria

Each presentation is scored by the audience on four dimensions, each out of 10:

| Criterion | What we're looking for |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Accuracy** | Does the output reflect the input data, or did it invent things? |
| **Safety** | Was the input properly anonymised? Would PECA 2016 still apply? |
| **Format** | Is the output ready to send / file / share, or does it need heavy editing? |
| **Time Saved** | Compared to doing this manually, what % time saved? (target: >80%) |

**Winning team criteria:** highest total across all four. Tie-breaker: which prompt would the room most likely steal and use tomorrow morning.

---

## 🏆 The takeaway

The best master prompts share three features:

1. **They are specific** — a generic "summarise this" loses to a specific 5-part prompt every time
2. **They include negative constraints** — what NOT to do (no emojis, no first-person, no invented data)
3. **They have a verification clause** — *"if unsure, use a placeholder"* — which keeps you safe

---

## 📝 After the activity — save your prompt

Add your final, refined master prompt to your personal prompt library. Recommended places:
- A note in OneNote or Notion
- A pinned message in your work WhatsApp Notes
- A bookmark called "My Master Prompts" in your browser

You will reuse this prompt **dozens of times**. The 30 minutes you spent today will pay back every Friday afternoon for the rest of your career.

---

## 💡 Bonus: the "Master of Master Prompts" pattern

Once you have 5-10 master prompts that work for you, write a **"prompt of prompts"** — a meta-prompt that you give the AI to help it tailor its outputs to your style. Example:

```text
For the rest of this conversation, please adopt the following defaults unless 
I say otherwise:
- I am a [ROLE] at a Pakistani [SECTOR] operator
- I write in British English with SI units
- I prefer outputs under 400 words unless I ask for more
- I want numbered lists for action items, tables for comparisons
- I want explicit placeholders [LIKE THIS] for missing data — never invented facts
- I want a one-line verification note when you make any uncertain claim
- No emojis. No "I hope this helps." No "let me know if you have questions."
```

Paste this once at the start of any session. The AI will follow it for the rest of the conversation.
