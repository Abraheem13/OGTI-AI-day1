## 📋 How This Activity Works

Welcome to the **"AI or Not AI?"** ice-breaker. This is the first activity of Day 1 — and it has no right or wrong answers. The goal is to **surface your team's assumptions** about what AI can and cannot do in Pakistan's Oil & Gas sector — not to test your knowledge.

### Format

- **Groups of 3–4** at your departmental table
- **15 minutes total** · phones away for the first 10 minutes
- Each table receives a card deck (also listed below)
- **Sort each task into one of two piles:**
  - ✅ **AI can do this reliably TODAY**
  - ❌ **AI cannot do this well yet**
- Each group nominates **ONE surprising placement** and explains **WHY** (2 minutes per group)
- The facilitator captures disagreements on the whiteboard — these become Day 1's recurring through-line

## 🧠 The Three Categories — Spoilers Below

> ⚠️ **Facilitators only — do not read this section to participants until after the discussion.**

<details>

### ✅ AI Can Reliably Do This Today
- **Task 1** — Pigging report summary (long-document reading)
- **Task 2** — Wellhead pressure anomaly detection (anomaly detection is a solved problem)
- **Task 5** — OGRA petition draft (generative drafting from notes)
- **Task 8** — Roman Urdu translation
- **Task 10** — Vendor bid comparison table (structured data analysis)
- **Task 12** — Predictive maintenance on pumps (Shell, Equinor proof points)
- **Task 13** — Gas theft pattern detection (anomaly detection on transactional data)
- **Task 15** — WhatsApp safety alert drafting
- **Task 16** — EPC tender risk extraction (NotebookLM use case)
- **Task 18** — Diesel demand forecasting
- **Task 20** — Daily rig report consolidation

### ❌ AI Cannot Do This Well Yet (or Should Not Be Allowed To)
- **Task 4** — Live shutdown decision (safety-critical, requires human accountability)
- **Task 6** — Toolbox talk (requires trust, presence, cultural fluency)
- **Task 9** — Sign-off with legal accountability (AI cannot be liable under OGRA / PPRA)
- **Task 11** — Face-to-face JV negotiation (relationships, body language, escalation)
- **Task 14** — Fatality investigation (empathy, ethics, accountability)
- **Task 17** — Board-level budget approval (judgement + political ownership)
- **Task 19** — Relationship building with regulators (irreducibly human)

### 🌗 The Grey Zone — Where the Best Debates Happen
- **Task 3** — PPRA Rule 36 bid evaluation: AI can *draft* it, but Pakistan's procurement rules require a named evaluator
- **Task 7** — Seismic interpretation: AI assists, but a geoscientist must validate before drilling commitment

## 🎤 Wrap-Up Questions for the Plenary

After the 15 minutes, the facilitator should ask the room:

1. **Which task surprised you the most when your group's verdict was challenged?**
2. **Was there a task your group split on? What was the disagreement actually about — the technology, the data quality, or the accountability?**
3. **Looking at the "AI cannot do this" pile — what do those tasks have in common?** (Hint: accountability, relationships, judgement under uncertainty.)
