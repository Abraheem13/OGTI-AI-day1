# ⚔️ Activity — The Tool Duel

**Time required:** 20 minutes
**Format:** Pairs (one person drives ChatGPT, the other drives Claude)
**Purpose:** Develop instinct for which tool to reach for in which situation

---

## 🎯 The premise

You and your partner are the same person, with the same task, at the same desk. The only difference: one of you can only use ChatGPT, the other can only use Claude. **Same prompt. Different tool. Compare.**

You will run **three rounds**, each on a different type of task. After each round, agree which tool won — and **why**.

---

## 🥇 Round 1 — The Quick Draft

**Task:** Draft a 100-word announcement that all confined-space entries on the refinery will require an additional gas-test sign-off, effective Monday.

**Prompt to use in both tools:**

```text
You are an HSE Manager at a Pakistani refinery. Write a 100-word internal 
announcement that, effective Monday, all confined-space entries require an 
additional independent gas-test sign-off by the shift HSE officer. 

Tone: professional, firm but supportive. British English. Suitable for posting 
on the plant notice board and sending to all shift WhatsApp groups. 

End with one specific phone number / email for queries — use [HSE-CONTACT] 
as placeholder.
```

**Time both:** Which one gave you a usable draft faster?

## 🥈 Round 2 — The Long Read

**Task:** Read a long, dense document and find the three biggest risks.

**Use the sample JV agreement from Module 2** (`02_chatgpt_vs_claude/sample_data/sample_jv_agreement.txt`).

**Prompt to use in both tools:**

```text
I'm pasting a draft JV agreement. Identify the THREE biggest risks for the 
Pakistani party. For each risk, cite the exact section number and quote 
the exact problematic text. Do not invent any clauses — if uncertain, say so.

[PASTE THE DOCUMENT]
```

**Watch:**
- Does ChatGPT's free tier read the WHOLE document, or just the start and end?
- Does Claude cite specific section numbers?
- Did either tool invent a clause that isn't in the document? (Ctrl-F to check)

## 🥉 Round 3 — The Nuanced Letter

**Task:** Draft a difficult letter — escalating to a regulator while preserving the relationship.

**Prompt to use in both tools:**

```text
You are a Senior Compliance Manager at a Pakistani downstream operator. 

Context: OGRA has asked us a clarification question about a routine filing. 
The question is based on a misunderstanding — what they think happened did 
not happen. We need to correct the record FIRMLY but RESPECTFULLY, without 
being defensive or appearing to evade the question.

Task: draft the response letter. Cover:
1. Polite acknowledgement of their query
2. Factual correction of the misunderstanding
3. Supporting evidence we are willing to share
4. Offer of a follow-up meeting or briefing
5. Reaffirmation of our commitment to compliance

Length: under 300 words.
Tone: respectful, professional, confident but not arrogant.
British English. Leave [ADDRESSEE], [REFERENCE], [DATE], [SIGNATORY] as 
placeholders. No first-person pronouns ("we" only, never "I").
```

**Watch:**
- Which one nailed the tone — firm without being defensive?
- Which one over-apologised or under-apologised?
- Which one would your CEO sign without changes?

## 💡 What the data usually shows

Across many trainings:
- **Round 1 (Quick draft):** Often a tie. ChatGPT slightly faster; Claude slightly more polished.
- **Round 2 (Long read):** Claude usually wins by a clear margin — bigger context window on free tier, more rigorous about citing.
- **Round 3 (Nuanced letter):** Claude usually wins on tone for formal / regulator-facing content; ChatGPT can be slightly punchier for internal communications.

**The lesson:** there is no universal winner. Build a **two-tab habit** — open both and run important prompts in both for 30 seconds of comparison.
