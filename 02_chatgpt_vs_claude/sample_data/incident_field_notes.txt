Inspector: [INSPECTOR-B]

Status: Near-miss, no injury, minor environmental impact contained
Classification: Category 3 (per internal scale; OGRA Section 26 likely applicable)

================================================================
WHAT HAPPENED (raw, verbatim from field log)
================================================================

- Time: approximately 14:18, weekday afternoon. Operator was [OPERATOR-A], 18 years' experience.
- Activity: routine pigging operation at receiver trap, [STATION-N], gas transmission line, diameter 24 inches, pressure rating 1000 psig.
- Reported sequence:
    1. Operator opened the trap door per SOP-PIG-04 after pressure-down was confirmed.
    2. Residual gas release was larger than expected — operator reported "smell stronger than usual, felt warm air."
    3. Gas detector on operator's belt alarmed at 18% LEL within 4 seconds.
    4. Operator stepped back to safe distance, alerted control room via handheld radio.
    5. Control room initiated emergency isolation procedure. Took 9 minutes from alarm to full isolation (procedure target: 5 minutes).
- No fire. No injury. Operator showed no symptoms — sent for precautionary medical check, cleared same day.
- Wind direction: westerly, light (~8 km/h). Vapour plume drifted away from main building toward open field — favourable.
- Estimated release: ~25 cubic metres of gas. Calculated from line volume and isolation timing.
- Three contractors were within 30 metres but downwind by approximately 90 degrees. They were instructed to evacuate by the operator personally.

================================================================
WHAT MAY HAVE GONE WRONG
================================================================

- The pressure-down indicator at the trap valve showed atmospheric, but the actual upstream check valve may have been partially seized. This is a working hypothesis pending physical inspection.
- Last functional test of the upstream check valve: 11 months ago. Schedule says 6-monthly.
- Operator followed the SOP correctly — this is NOT a procedural compliance issue at the operator level.
- The 9-minute response time vs 5-minute target points to a control-room procedure gap. Initial review suggests:
    * Radio handover protocol was unclear — the duty controller was busy with another task
    * Emergency isolation valve actuator showed slow response time — may need maintenance
- Contractors were within the gas exclusion zone — a procedural breach. The contractor supervisor had been briefed but the briefing was 3 days prior, not 24 hours.

================================================================
IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN
================================================================

- Operator [OPERATOR-A] medically cleared and stood down for the remainder of shift
- Pig launch suspended for the entire line
- Upstream check valve isolated, line de-pressured fully
- Contractors withdrawn from station
- Incident response team mobilised within 12 minutes
- GM Operations briefed verbally at 15:30 same day
- Internal incident form 02-A initiated; OGRA disclosure draft started

================================================================
RECOMMENDED CAPA (corrective and preventive actions)
================================================================

- Immediate: physical inspection and pressure-test of upstream check valve
- Within 7 days: review of all check valves overdue for functional testing across the line
- Within 14 days: control-room radio handover protocol revised; tabletop exercise scheduled
- Within 30 days: contractor pre-job briefing made mandatory within 24h of any pigging activity
- Within 60 days: emergency isolation valve actuators inspected across the network

================================================================
INSPECTOR'S PERSONAL OBSERVATIONS (not for external disclosure)
================================================================

- The operator's quick reaction — stepping back at 18% LEL rather than continuing — likely prevented a more serious outcome
- The control room response time gap is concerning and may indicate workload / staffing issues
- Maintenance backlog is real. Check-valve schedule slippage is not unique to this station.
- Recommend GM Operations raises this with Director (Maintenance) — pattern issue, not one-station issue.
